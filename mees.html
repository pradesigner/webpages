1. Hyper Extended Hours Effect

HEHE uses the movement during non trading hours for a regular stock to determine whether to enter its equivalent CDR. The system is as follows:

  1. From the list of NE CDRs below, examine extended hours trading of equivalent USD stock on YF.
  2. If the USD stock price had been going U|D, place stop-limit B|S on the CDR at above|below closing price.
  3. The CDR will try to 'catchup' to the USD stock and a quick (possibly substantial) profit is practically guaranteed. If it looks as though the price keep going in the same direction (U|D), an exit can be placed near breakeven so that the profits can continue.
  4. Exception: do not ever trade against the larger USD tread. Only place the CDR trade if following conditions are met: if USD stock trend is U|D, and extended hours is U|D, then place CDR trade as B|S
  5. Trading against the larger trend can work, but it is more likely that the extended hours activity will be wiped out by it, so quick entry/exit may be successful.
  6. Here are the types of orders that can be used:
    • market buy|sell: market M orders are executed as close to the market price as possible
    • stop-limit B|S: place SL, M<SL | M>SL (buy at higher SL, sell at a lower SL)
    • limit B|S: place L. M>L | M<L (buy at a lower L, sell at a higher L)
